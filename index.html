The volume, velocity, and variety at which data are now becoming available allow us to study urban environments based on human behavior at a spatial, temporal, and thematic granularity that was not achievable until now. Such data-driven approaches opens up additional, complementary perspectives on how urban systems function, especially if they are based on User-Generated Con- tent (UGC). While the data sources, e.g., social media, introduce specific biases, they also open up new possibilities for scientists and the broader public. For in- stance, they provide answers to questions that previously could only be addressed by complex simulations or extensive human participant surveys.
